The document provides a detailed overview of Medicare payment systems for ambulatory surgical centers (ASCs) and hospital outpatient departments, focusing on how these services are billed and paid under the Medicare program. Medicare payments for ASCs are structured under a **9-payment group** system, where each group is assigned a predetermined rate based on similar costs but not necessarily clinical similarity. ASCs receive payment for each procedure in a group at the same rate, and if multiple procedures are performed simultaneously, they are paid for each. The procedure with the highest payment rate receives 100% of the payment, while each additional procedure receives 50% of the payment. Medicare covers direct costs such as nursing, technician services, drugs, supplies, anesthesia, and diagnostic services, but does not include implantable devices or prosthetics, which are billed separately. Professional services, such as those by physicians or anesthesiologists, are billed directly by the provider to Medicare.

Under the **Prospective Payment System (OPPS)**, established in 2000, hospital outpatient departments receive payment for each of the approximately 5,500 services assigned to an APC group. All services in a group are paid the same rate, and the hospital receives 100% of the payment for the procedure with the highest rate, with 50% for each additional procedure, subject to exceptions. Payment under OPPS is intended to cover the costs of procedures, and ASCs do not include professional services in their Medicare payments, which are billed separately. Under OPPS, ASCs may bill separately for additional services directly related to the procedure, such as certain drugs and diagnostic services, but not for implantable devices. They may also bill for non-directly related services like laboratory tests.

Since 1990, payment rates have been updated to reflect inflation, but no revisions were made using ASC cost data until 1994. In 1994, Congress mandated a survey of ASC costs by CMS, and CMS conducted a survey in 1994 to collect cost data.
